Bathroom Rankings

About Me

When I came to BYU, my brother was also still studying here, and I was able to do a lot of things with him over the course of my Freshman and Sophomore years. At one point, when my Sophomore year was about to start, my brother issued a challenge to me. He wanted to have a competition to see who could visit the most bathrooms on campus during the year. I declined because I thought it was ridiculous and would be a pain to find all these bathrooms when I was in a hurry to get to my classes. But I soon came to my senses, and accepted his challenge. I don't think he ended up doing too much with it, but I kept a detailed list of every bathroom I visited on campus. It has become quite extensive, and actually became quite useful. If I'm late to class but need to use the bathroom, I can easily direct my path to class to include the most convenient bathroom. I know exactly where they are, and by extension, I also know where the drinking fountains are. As I continued this process, I found that not all the bathrooms were equal. Some stood out as clearly better than the rest. Here is my top 5 list, with some reasons why they are among the elite:

  1. Eyring Science Center 4th floor: C453A
    • Peaceful - few people use it; poop worry free
    • Bright - lots of natural light; sometimes that's all the sun a college student can hope for
    • Hidden - feels like a secret and everybody likes to know secrets
  2. Bensen Building Basement: W013
    • Massive - this room is huge; it feels like the entire building could fit in there
    • Convenient - it's in one of the most travelled buildings on campus
    • Lots of stalls - take your time, there's nobody waiting
  3. Nichols Building Single Person: 102, 106
    • High Quality - modern with lots of light (1000000000% nicer than your apartment bathroom)
    • Convenient - located near one of the most popular walkways on campus
    • Private - no need to worry about the awkward bathroom interactions
  4. Herald R. Clark Building Basement: 138
    • Feels Exciting - There's a slight element of fear from the step up into the restroom and the big caution sign where you can put a dent in your head as you come out of the stall.
    • Your Own World - The main stall has the ceiling lowered just outside, making it feel like your own little cave of privacy.
  5. Eyring Science Center Underground Labs: U131
    • Secret - Located in one of the coolest places on campus. It feels like a restricted mad scientist area, but it's open to everyone
    • Convenience - Next to the elevators, and near the center of campus.

Here's my brother for ya!
